Egg Free Chocolate Chip Pumpkin Cookies<ING>• 2 cups all-purpose flour• 2 teaspoons baking powder• 1 teaspoon baking soda• 1/2 teaspoon salt• 1/2 cup butter, softened• 1 cup white sugar• 1 egg• 1 teaspoon vanilla extract• 1 cup canned pumpkin puree• 1/2 cup raisins• 1/4 cup chopped walnuts• 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder• 1/4 cup flaked coconut• 1/4 cup peanut butter• 2 tablespoons light corn syrup• 1/4 cup butter, softened• 1/2 cup white sugar• 1 teaspoon vanilla extract• 1/4 cup packed brown sugar• 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour• 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon• 1 teaspoon baking powder• 1 cup chopped walnuts• 1/2 cup chopped cocoa powder• 1/4 cup flaked coconut<INS>‣ Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Lightly grease cookie sheets.‣ In a large bowl, cream together the butter, 1 cup white sugar, and egg. Beat in the vanilla. Combine the flour, baking soda, salt, and baking powder; stir into the creamed mixture. Finally, stir in the pumpkin and raisins. Drop dough by rounded spoonfuls onto the prepared cookie sheets.‣ Bake for 8 to 10 minutes in the preheated oven. Allow cookies to cool on baking sheet for 5 minutes before removing to a wire rack to cool completely.‣ In a medium bowl, mix together 1/4 cup melted butter, corn syrup, 1/4 cup butter, 1/2 cup white sugar, and vanilla. Stir in the flour mixture alternately with the remaining 1/4 cup of brown sugar. Stir in the cinnamon and baking powder. Spread the mixture evenly over the cooled cookies.
