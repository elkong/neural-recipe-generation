Granny's Beef Stew<ING>• 2 tablespoons olive oil• 1 large onion, chopped• 1 pound boneless beef stew meat, cut into 1 inch cubes• 2 cups water• 1 (15 ounce) can kidney beans, rinsed and drained• 1 (15 ounce) can whole kernel corn, drained• 2 potatoes, peeled and cubed• 1 cup chopped celery• 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper• 1/2 teaspoon salt• 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper• 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour• 1 1/2 cups beef broth<INS>‣ Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Cook and stir the onion in the hot oil until the onion has softened and turned translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the beef and cook until no longer pink and the meat is beginning to brown on the outside, about 10 minutes.‣ Pour the water into the pot and bring to a boil. Stir in the kidney beans, corn, potatoes, celery, and black pepper. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer until the vegetables are tender, about 20 minutes.‣ Season with salt, black pepper, and salt and pepper to taste. Stir in the flour to form a roux; cook and stir until the roux has begun to brown, about 5 minutes. Remove from heat and stir in the beef broth.‣ Transfer the stew to a serving platter, cover, and refrigerate until the meat is ready, about 2 hours.
