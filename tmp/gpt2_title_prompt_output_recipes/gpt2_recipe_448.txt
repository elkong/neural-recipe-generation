Aaron's Missouri Burger<ING>• 2 tablespoons olive oil• 1 clove garlic, minced• 1 teaspoon onion powder• 1 teaspoon ground black pepper• 1/4 teaspoon salt• 1 (15 ounce) can kidney beans, rinsed and drained• 1 cup uncooked long grain white rice• 1 pound ground beef• 1 cup chopped celery• 1 cup chopped onion• 1/2 cup chopped green bell pepper• 1/2 cup chopped celery<INS>‣ Heat the olive oil in a skillet over medium heat, and stir in the garlic, onion powder, black pepper, and salt. Cook and stir until the garlic softens, and the onion has softened, about 5 minutes.‣ Stir the kidney beans and rice into the skillet, and simmer until the rice is tender, 15 to 20 minutes.‣ Meanwhile, heat the beef and celery in a large skillet over medium heat, and cook and stir until the beef is crumbly, no longer pink, and no longer pink, about 10 minutes. Drain and discard any excess grease.‣ Stir the onion, green bell pepper, and celery into the skillet, and continue cooking until the vegetables are tender and the flavors have blended, about 10 minutes. Season with salt and pepper, and mix into the chili.
